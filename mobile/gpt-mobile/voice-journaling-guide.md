# Voice Journaling Guide

Voice journaling is useful for quick capture, especially when typing is too slow. Keep the workflow simple and private.

## Good Voice Entries

Short entries are easier to review later:

- One thought.
- One situation.
- One feeling.
- One question.
- One next step.

Timestamped thoughts are often more useful than long transcripts.

## Suggested Dictation Pattern

```text
Thought: [one or two sentences]
Mood:
Energy:
Intensity:
Next step:
```

## Safety Boundary

Do not send crisis content into automation without human support. If you are in immediate danger, may act on self-harm urges, cannot stay safe, or are at risk from someone else, prioritize trusted human support, emergency services when danger may be imminent, and crisis resources such as 988 in the U.S.

## Privacy Boundary

- Use phone lock and account protection.
- Avoid dictating private content near other people.
- Avoid shared devices.
- Review transcription mistakes before sending text to an AI app.
- Avoid screenshots of private entries.
- Be careful with cloud sync and backups.
- Do not use untrusted plugins or shortcut actions for sensitive journal data.

## Reflection Loop

1. Dictate a short thought.
2. Save it with a timestamp in Obsidian Mobile.
3. Later, copy only a selected excerpt into ChatGPT mobile.
4. Paste useful reflection back into Obsidian after review.
