# Safety Model

The safety model has two top-level modes: reflection mode and safety mode.

## Reflection Mode

Use reflection mode when the entry contains distress but no immediate safety indicators. The agent may summarize, label emotions, identify possible patterns, ask questions, and suggest small next steps.

## Safety Mode

Use safety mode when the entry suggests possible imminent danger, suicidal intent, self-harm preparation, inability to stay safe, threats from others, abuse, severe intoxication, psychosis or mania indicators, eating-disorder medical danger, or major functional collapse.

Safety mode prioritizes immediate human connection, emergency or crisis resources, reducing access to harm, and stopping deep analysis.

## Non-Scoring Rule

Do not assign numerical risk scores. Use routing language such as `reflection`, `support_encouraged`, `safety_check`, or `urgent_support`.
